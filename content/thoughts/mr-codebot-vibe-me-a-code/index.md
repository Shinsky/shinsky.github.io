There’s a pervasive distrust of AI tools among the people I care about. I share their concerns that AI companies are gobbling up art and culture without permission in service of creating useless slop. 

But if there’s one thing I am on board with using AI tools for, it’s coding. Despite having used computers since I was a child, and having a well above average understanding of how they work, I’ve never picked up any desire to code. And that’s fine! But when I started dabbling with Claude — making this site, using it to help compile research, and create a basic transit time web app — I wanted to see how far I could push it.

![](podsync%20claude%20prompt.png "right  | The prompt I gave to Claude when starting to build PodSync - Screenshot: Andrew Lashinsky")

So I asked Claude to help me build an app that could sync media to my iPods, Zunes, and other portable devices. PodSync is what I came up with (I thought the AI-chosen original “PodBay” was simply bad). While the project started as a way to work out Anthropic’s Fable 5 model, it’s blown my mind to see just how well an online tool can build a fully featured app, fix bugs, and iterate on command.

I now have an app that does 75% of what I need it to (so far). And spending all of my tokens polishing and adding to it has become a reliable distraction from life’s occasional lows.

Knowing the damage the AI industry is inflicting on the world, I still feel some form of guilt about my vibe-coding journey. I guess now I can just drown those thoughts out with music and movies on a 2 inch screen.
