Ripping your owned media isn't hard. I've been doing it since I was a kid! Once you get the process down, the most difficult thing is figuring out where to store the mountains of data you're creating. So here's a breakdown of the software and hardware tools I use.
## DVDs & Blu-rays
I use MakeMKV to rip movies and shows off of their respective discs. It works on pretty much any disc (DVD/Blu-ray/UHD Blu-ray) as long as you have a supported drive.

#### Disc Drives:
- Verbatim 71094 External All-in-One Optical Writer (DVD/Blu-ray)
- LG BP50NB40 Slim External Drive (DVD/Blu-ray/UHD Blu-ray)
#### Software:
- MakeMKV (ripping discs)
- MKVToolNix (creating, altering, and inspecting MKVs)

Once you have a drive and the MakeMKV software it's pretty self explanatory!

#### Some tips though:
- You can set MakeMKV to ignore titles under a certain duration (I go with 120 seconds). This is helpful for preventing the app from picking up small videos used for DVD menus and the like.
- The MakeMKV forums are, unsurprisingly, a great starting point for finding a drive or learning how to use the software!

## CDs
For audio discs the hardware is pretty much the same as for video discs... my Blu-ray drives all work flawlessly with CDs. The difference though is in the software.

I love free and open source stuff, but I'm not against paying for a decent product. dBpoweramp is worth the price for me. It's able to grab the audio from a disc, compare it to known good rips for your drive, and help you tag the files correctly.

I also have used the free app XLD on macOS to accomplish much of the same from time to time.

As for format: I rip tracks inALAC for its iPod/iPhone compatibility.

#### Software: 
- dBpoweramp (paid)
- XLD (free)
